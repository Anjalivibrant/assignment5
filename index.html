About Jamshedpur


Well known as the "Steel City of India", Jamshedpur derives its name from its founder Jamshedji Tata who had the vision of a bright future in making it a well planned industrial city. His son Dorabji Tata set up India’s 1st iron and steel company here during the British Colonial rule. Jamshedpur is also known by its fascinating nicknames of Tatanagar or simply Tata. Jamshedpur is completely maintained by Tata Steel for the most part. Tata Steel is responsible for the infrastructure as well the public services offered in most parts of the city.

P&M Mall

P&M Hi-Tech City Centre Mall in the city of Jamshedpur, Jharkhand, is the one of the largest shopping malls in eastern India. It has a gross lease-able (retail) area of 600,000 sq ft Film director Prakash Jha & R.K.Aggarwal are the builders of the mall.It is located at Outer Circle Road, Bistupur.

Tatanagar Junction

Tatanagar Junction railway station, station code TATA, is the main railway station serving the city of Jamshedpur in the Indian state of Jharkhand. It is located on the Howrah–Nagpur–Mumbai line of the Indian Railways. It has 6 platforms and handles around 100 trains each day.

TATA Steel

Tata Steel Limited is an Indian multinational steel-making company, based in Jamshedpur, Jharkhand and headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra. It is a part of the Tata Group. It was formerly known as Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited (TISCO)

Arka Jain University

ARKA JAIN University, is fastest growing university of Jharkhand and the 1st Private University of Kolhan Region, is a unit of JAIN Group, Bengaluru.

Nature Trail

Jamshedpur Nature Trail is yet another milestone achieved by the Steel City towards sustainable development. The project was initiated in collaboration with Rotary Club of Jamshedpur West and Grow-trees.com, for mass plantation of 12000 trees.

Jubilee Park

Jubilee Park is an urban park located in Jamshedpur, Jharkhand, India. This local attraction was opened to the public in 1958, on the occasion of the city's golden jubilee, the 50th anniversary of establishment. This park was originally a gift to the town of Jamshedpur from the Tata Steel Company.

Connectivity

  • By Railways- The railway station is known as Tatanagar JN.
  • By Bus- Regular bus services are available to Jamshedpur from Ranchi, Patna, Gaya, Kolkata, and Hazaribag.
  • By Car- Jamshedpur is connected by road with most of the major cities in India. NH 32 connects Jamshedpur to Govindpur on NH 2 (Kolkata-Delhi) via Chandil, Chas and Dhanbad. NH 33 connects Chandil to Barhi on NH 2 (Kolkata-Delhi) via Bundu, Ranchi, Ramgarh and Hazaribagh.
  • By Plane- There are no commercial flights to Jamshedpur airport which is restricted to chartered planes of the TATA group.
